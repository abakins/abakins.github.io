Some areas of current or prior research are listed below. I'm using first person language here, but I work with many wonderful collaborators on these projects! 

# Solar System Radio Astronomy 

I use passive microwave observations of solar system objects to study their atmosphere and surface properties. I mostly work with observations from large radio interferometers, such as the Very Large Array, Atacama Large Millimeter Array, and the Giant Meterwave Radio Telescope, and I've been most recently focused on studies of Venus and the ice giants Uranus and Neptune. 

Some highlights include: 
- Evidence of a Polar Cyclone on Uranus from VLA Obserbations (2023, [paper](https://doi.org/10.1029/2023GL102872), [press](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/nasa-scientists-make-first-observation-of-a-polar-cyclone-on-uranus))

- Complications in the ALMA Detection of Phosphine on Venus (2021, [paper](https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/abd56a), [press](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/life-on-venus-claim-faces-strongest-challenge-yet/)) 


# Radio Occultations 

I also work on simulation and analysis methods for radio occultations of terrestrial and giant planet atmospheres, with a specific focus on retrieving parameters of interest from the radio signal attenuation. I am an affiliate member of both the EnVision Radio Science Experiment and the JUICE-PRIDE radio occultation teams. During my graduate studies, I made laboratory measurements of the microwave opacity of gases relevant to these experiments.

Some highlights include: 

- Approaches for Retrieving Sulfur Species Abundances from Dual X–Ka-band Radio Occultations of Venus with EnVision and VERITAS (2023, [paper](https://doi.org/10.3847/PSJ/accae3), [press](https://aasnova.org/2023/05/10/using-spacecraft-signals-to-measure-sulfur-species-on-venus/))


# Earth Science Applications of Microwave Radiometry 

I'm interested in both analysis of pre-existing satellite passive microwave observations of Earth's surface and atmosphere and in advocating for the inclusion of next-generation radiometer technology on future space missions. I work with the SMAP ocean salinity products team at JPL and I conduct observing system simulations for exploratory mission designs, such as wideband L-band measurements of ocean salinity/vector winds and ice sheet temperature. 

Some highlights include: 

- Simulation Framework and Case Studies for the Design of Sea Surface Salinity Remote Sensing Missions (2023, [paper](https://doi.org/10.1109/JSTARS.2023.3234407))
